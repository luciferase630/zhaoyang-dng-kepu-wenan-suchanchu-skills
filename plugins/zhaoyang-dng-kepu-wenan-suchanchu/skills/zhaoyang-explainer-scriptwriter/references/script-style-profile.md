# Script Style Profile

This reference abstracts the writing style from completed public-education script and Excalidraw pairs. Apply the method to any topic; do not bind the style to gold, silver, oil, social insurance, or finance.

## Overall Feel

- Dense but spoken.
- Direct judgment first, explanation immediately after.
- Teaching-board rhythm: one idea, one beat, one transition.
- The speaker sounds like they are trying to prevent the viewer from being fooled by an easy story.
- The script can be long, but each paragraph should have a job.

## Opening Pattern

Start with one of these moves:

- Put the conclusion first: "我先把结论放在前面..."
- Name the bad decision: "你不应该因为一个短期刺激就..."
- Break the common shortcut: "很多人会把这件事简单理解成..."
- Set the learning promise: "我接下来把这个东西从定义、机制和风险讲明白。"

The opening should not wander. It should tell the viewer why this topic matters and what wrong path to avoid.

## Argument Skeleton

Use this skeleton for most scripts:

1. Conclusion or warning.
2. Common misunderstanding.
3. Basic definition.
4. Mechanism: why it works this way.
5. Concrete example, historical node, chart, number, or formula.
6. Correction: "所以真正关键不是 X，而是 Y."
7. Boundary: when the conclusion does not apply.
8. Final takeaway.

## Sentence Patterns

Use these patterns naturally, not mechanically:

- "我先把结论放前面。"
- "问题在于..."
- "普通人最容易误会的是..."
- "真正核心不是 X，而是 Y."
- "这句话听起来对，但它漏掉了一个前提。"
- "你要先分清楚..."
- "如果你只看 X，你会得到一个非常危险的结论。"
- "所以我们不能直接从 A 推到 B."
- "这不是投资建议，我只负责把逻辑讲清楚。"

## Pacing

- Use short paragraphs, often 1-3 sentences.
- Long paragraphs are acceptable only when walking through a mechanism step by step.
- Put key claims in their own paragraphs.
- After every abstract concept, add a concrete anchor: example, number, timeline, policy clause, formula, or daily-life scene.
- When a section becomes too dense, add a plain-language checkpoint: "说人话就是..."

## Evidence Handling

- Evidence should support a claim, not decorate the script.
- Introduce charts and screenshots with a question: "这张图到底说明什么？"
- Do not overread a chart. State what it proves and what it cannot prove.
- Put dates, laws, formulas, and named concepts in clean short lines so they can be reused on the Excalidraw canvas.

## Risk And Boundary Tone

For finance, policy, or welfare topics:

- Say clearly when the script is not investment advice.
- Separate long-term logic from short-term price movement.
- Separate 制度 rules from individual decision-making.
- Avoid "一定赚钱", "稳赚", "无脑买", and similar claims.
- If making a strong judgment, name the condition under which it could be wrong.

## What To Avoid

- Article-style literature review.
- Internet-marketing opening hooks.
- Empty emotional slogans.
- A pile of definitions before the viewer knows the central problem.
- Topic-specific imitation that copies old examples instead of copying the explanatory method.
